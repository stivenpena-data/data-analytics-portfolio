## Key Findings

### 1. Budget Overruns Concentrated in Two Areas
IT and Product departments account for 71% of total budget variance. The Mobile App v2.0 (P006) and CRM Integration (P011) together carry $75K in projected overruns. Root cause: scope changes approved mid-project without formal budget amendments.

### 2. On-Time Performance is the Critical Gap
Only 5 of 9 completed projects (56%) were delivered on time. Average delay for late projects: 6.8 days. HR and Finance projects delivered on time 100% of the time — their smaller scope and stable requirements are the differentiator.

### 3. High-Risk Projects Carry Disproportionate Budget
6 high-risk projects hold $1.33M (26%) of total portfolio budget. Three of these (P006, P011, P023) are in active execution with budget variance already above 5%.

### 4. Data & Analytics Department Leads Efficiency
Stiven Pena's projects average 51% completion at 52% of planned budget spend — the best cost-efficiency ratio across all PMs. Recommendation: apply this team's estimation methodology to high-risk projects.

---

## Recommendations

1. **Implement formal change control** for scope changes — any scope addition requires budget amendment within 5 business days.
2. **Add weekly risk reviews** for At Risk and On Hold projects.
3. **Replicate HR/Finance project planning** practices (smaller milestones, tighter scope definition) across IT and Product.
4. **Set 80% on-time delivery as Q3 2024 OKR** with PM-level accountability.
